Phase 3 — AI / OCR Field Extraction
System automatically extracts and structures key data from every uploaded document
🤖 AI / System
1
OCR Processing
Uploaded PDF/image is run through OCR engine. Supports Tamil + English bilingual text. Raw text layer is stored on the Document record.
2
Structured Field Extraction
AI model extracts typed fields from the document based on its type. Each field is stored with a confidence score (0–100%).
EC Fields Extracted
  • Survey Number & Extent
  • All encumbrance entries
  • Mortgage holders & amounts
  • Discharge dates
  • Certificate period covered
Sale Deed Fields Extracted
  • Seller & buyer names
  • Sale consideration amount
  • Registration number & date
  • Schedule of property (area)
  • Stamp duty & registration fees
3
Auto-Risk Detection Pass
After extraction, the system runs a set of rule-based checks across all documents for the parcel and generates preliminary Risk Flag cards. These surface in the Document Reader's right column and in Phase 5.
Active mortgage detected → Risk flag NRI owner detected → Risk flag Missing 30-yr EC period → Risk flag

Phase 4 — Document Review & Annotation
Lawyer reads, verifies extractions, and annotates every document
⚖️ Legal Advisor
1
Select Parcel from Left Panel
Legal Advisor clicks a parcel card in the left panel. Parcel status changes to In Review. Centre panel loads the Documents tab with a grid of all uploaded documents for this parcel (colour-coded: green = verified, amber = pending, red = issue).
2
Open Document Reader Modal
Clicking a document card opens the full-screen Document Reader modal — a 3-column workspace:
Col 1 — PDF Viewer (35%)
  • Render full PDF / image
  • Text search & highlight
  • Page nav, zoom
  • Tamil/English toggle
  • Annotate: select text → choose colour
Col 2 — Extracted Fields (40%)
  • AI-extracted field + value
  • Confidence % badge
  • Lawyer can override value
  • Mark field as verified ✓
  • Linked annotations appear here
Col 3 — Risk Flags (25%)
  • Auto-detected risks for this doc
  • Accept / Dismiss / Escalate
  • Severity indicator (High/Med/Low)
3
Annotate Clauses in PDF
Lawyer selects text in PDF, picks an annotation colour, and adds a note. Annotations are saved to DocumentAnnotation records.
🔴 Risk — Legal issue requiring action
🟡 Query — Unclear, needs clarification
🟢 Verified — Confirmed correct
🔵 Condition — Subject to condition
4
Verify or Override Extracted Fields
For each extracted field, the lawyer reviews the AI value and confidence score. If confidence is <70%, lawyer is prompted to verify manually. Lawyer can type a corrected value — the override is logged in the audit trail.
≥90% confidence → Auto-highlighted for quick confirm <70% confidence → Mandatory lawyer override
5
Use AI Legal Bot for Clause Insights
On any clause, lawyer clicks "Ask AI" → a contextual popup shows relevant Tamil Nadu statutes, Madras High Court precedents, and sample language. The AI chat allows custom questions about the document.
TN-specific statute references Madras HC case snippets Similar property language samples
5
Phase 5 — Risk Analysis & Escalation
Lawyer reviews auto-detected risks and decides: Accept, Dismiss, or Escalate
⚖️ Legal Advisor 🏢 Portfolio Manager 🤖 System
🔴 High Risk Auto-Flags
  • Active mortgage / undischarged lien
  • Missing EC period (gap in 30 years)
  • Court case / eCourts litigation hit
  • NRI owner without valid POA
  • Forest / wetland / coastal restriction
🟡 Medium Risk Auto-Flags
  • Partial release of mortgage
  • NRI ownership (POA present)
  • Area discrepancy between EC & Patta
  • Revenue records not matching survey
  • Ownership gap (>5 years undocumented)
🟢 Low Risk / Informational
  • Name spelling variation (minor)
  • Old satisfied mortgage (discharged)
  • Non-material area rounding difference
  • HUF ownership (documentation adequate)
1
Review Risk Flag Cards
Each risk card shows: severity badge, description, source document reference, and three action buttons. Lawyer must action every flag before checklist Phase 5 (Final) can be completed.
2
Action Every Risk Flag
For each risk, the lawyer chooses one of three actions:
🔀 Risk Flag Decision
✓ Accept

Risk acknowledged; not blocking (e.g. old discharged mortgage). Lawyer adds note. Flag marked accepted, parcel continues.

✗ Dismiss

Flag not applicable to this parcel (e.g. false positive from OCR). Lawyer explains dismissal in note.

⚠ Escalate

Blocking issue (active mortgage, NRI POA missing). Moves to Escalation Queue. Parcel status → Flagged. Portfolio Manager notified.

⚠️
Escalation Sub-Flow (Critical Issues)

1. Lawyer escalates risk → moves to Escalation Queue
2. Portfolio Manager receives notification
3. Site Manager contacts relevant authority (bank for NOC, revenue dept for name correction, etc.)
4. Clarification document uploaded to Doc Repository
5. Lawyer reviews uploaded doc, marks escalation "Resolved" with a note
6. Parcel risk flag removed; workflow can continue

Phase 6 — Cross-Document Comparison
Compare extracted fields across all documents to detect inconsistencies
⚖️ Legal Advisor 🤖 System
1
Run Consistency Check (Auto)
The Consistency Check tab auto-runs when the lawyer switches to it. System compares shared fields across all documents for the parcel (owner name, area, survey number, registration dates) and generates a consistency report.
✓ Match — consistent across all docs ⚠ Minor variation — likely typo/spelling ✗ Mismatch — requires clarification
2
Manual Compare Tab (Side-by-Side)
Lawyer selects 2 or more documents in the Compare tab. System shows a side-by-side table of extracted fields. Mismatched cells are highlighted in red. Confidence scores shown per value.
Common Discrepancy Types
  • Owner name spelling mismatch
  • Area figures differ (acres vs sqft rounding)
  • Survey number sub-division notation
  • Registration date vs execution date
Lawyer Actions on Discrepancy
  • Mark "Acceptable Variation" (minor)
  • Mark "Needs Clarification" → yellow Query annotation
  • Add note explaining judgment call
  • Trigger query to Site Manager
3
Query Resolution Sub-Flow
When "Needs Clarification" is selected, a Query Annotation is created and Site Manager is notified via the notification bell. Query appears in a "Pending Queries" list in the right panel.
💬
Query Resolution Sub-Flow (Document Discrepancy)

1. Lawyer marks discrepancy "Needs Clarification" → yellow annotation created
2. System adds to "Pending Queries" in right panel; Site Manager notified
3. Site Manager uploads clarification document (e.g. name confirmation from revenue office)
4. Lawyer reviews uploaded doc in Document Reader
5. Lawyer marks query "Resolved"; annotation updated to green "Verified"
6. Checklist item advances; consistency check re-runs

Phase 7 — Property History Review
30-year ownership and encumbrance timeline analysis
⚖️ Legal Advisor
1
Open Property History Tab
System renders a vertical interactive timeline merging: ownership transfers (sale deeds, gift deeds, inheritance) and encumbrance events (mortgage creation, partial release, full discharge) over 30 years.
2
Verify Ownership Chain Continuity
Lawyer checks that ownership chain is unbroken: every "To" owner in a transfer matches the "From" owner in the next transfer. Any gap year is highlighted in amber.
Timeline Event Types
  • 🔵 Sale (registered sale deed)
  • 🟢 Gift / Transfer
  • 🟡 Inheritance / Partition
  • 🔴 Mortgage created
  • ⚫ Mortgage discharged
Key Checks
  • No ownership gaps >5 years
  • All mortgages have discharge events
  • Current PATTA owner = last transfer "To"
  • No overlapping ownership periods
3
Click Events for Detail
Each timeline node is clickable — opens a detail popup with the linked document, all extracted fields for that event, and any annotations. Lawyer can drag the timeline to compare two periods side-by-side.

Phase 8 — Structured Checklist Verification
5-phase gated workflow — each phase must be complete before the next unlocks
⚖️ Legal Advisor
1
Documents
All required doc types uploaded? Legible? Correct period?
2
Ownership
All owners verified? Transfer chain complete? NRI compliance?
3
Encumbrances
All mortgages identified? NOCs obtained? Lien discharges confirmed?
4
Compliance
TN legal requirements met? Forest/wetland clear? Registration valid?
5
Final Review
All risks addressed? All queries resolved? Ready for opinion?
Verdict Options per Checklist Item:  ✓ Verified  — confirmed correct  |  ⚠ Queried  — pending clarification (blocks completion)  |  ∅ Waived  — not applicable (lawyer adds reason)
Gate Rule: If any Phase 1–4 item has an unresolved Query verdict, Phase 5 is locked. If Phase 5 is incomplete, the Opinion tab's Draft & Sign controls are disabled. This is a hard system enforcement.
1
Complete Each Phase Sequentially
Lawyer works through phases in order. For each checklist item: read the description, refer to relevant document/annotation, choose verdict (Verified / Queried / Waived), optionally add a note. Progress shown as "3/7 items" per phase.
2
Checklist Completion Unlocks Opinion
Once all 5 phases are fully verified/waived, the system auto-unlocks the Opinion tab. Parcel's completionScore reaches 100. Right panel shows all phases as ✓ Done. Opinion drafting button activates.
completionScore = 100 → Opinion tab unlocked
9
Phase 9 — Legal Opinion Drafting
Lawyer drafts, reviews, and approves each section of the legal opinion
⚖️ Legal Advisor 🤖 AI / System
1
Open Opinion Tab (now unlocked)
System presents the opinion workspace with 5 pre-defined sections. Each section has an AI-generated draft (shown with purple left-border as "AI Generated"). Lawyer must review and explicitly accept each section.
Opinion Sections
  • 1. Title & Property Description
  • 2. Document Findings
  • 3. Risks & Encumbrances
  • 4. Legal Verdict
  • 5. Terms & Conditions
Section States
  • Draft — AI-generated, unreviewed
  • Editing — Lawyer is modifying
  • Accepted ✓ — Lawyer approved
  • (All 5 accepted → sign enabled)
2
Edit & Accept Each Section
For each section: click "Edit" to enter free-text editing mode (editable div). Lawyer writes/modifies the content. Clicks "Accept" to lock the section green. A section can be un-accepted and re-edited before the final sign. Accepted sections show a green border.
3
Choose Final Verdict
In the right panel "Send Report" card, lawyer selects the overall verdict before signing:
📋 Overall Legal Verdict
✅ Safe to Proceed

Title is clear. No blocking encumbrances. Recommend proceeding with acquisition.

⚠ Proceed with Caution

Minor issues noted. Conditions attached. Recommend proceeding subject to conditions.

🚫 Do Not Proceed

Blocking legal issues identified. Title not clear. Recommend not acquiring.

10
Phase 10 — Digital Sign-off & Finalization
Lawyer digitally signs the opinion — this is irreversible and locks the record
⚖️ Legal Advisor 🤖 System
1
Click "Sign & Finalise"
With all 5 sections accepted and a verdict selected, the "Sign & Finalise" button becomes active. Clicking it triggers a confirmation dialog: "You are about to digitally sign this opinion. This action cannot be undone."
2
System Applies Digital Signature
System computes a digital signature hash over the opinion content, lawyer identity, and timestamp. Stored on LegalOpinion.signature. The locked = true flag is set.
What Gets Locked
  • Opinion content (no edits possible)
  • All 5 section texts
  • Final verdict
  • Lawyer identity + timestamp
System Updates
  • Parcel status → Completed
  • LegalOpinion.locked = true
  • signedAt = current timestamp
  • Audit trail: "Opinion signed"
  • Portfolio Manager notified
3
Export Signed Opinion PDF
Lawyer (or Portfolio Manager) exports the opinion as a watermarked, locked PDF. PDF includes: lawyer name watermark, signed timestamp, digital signature verification string. Copy-paste of content is disabled.
Watermark: lawyer name + timestamp PDF content copy-paste disabled Signature hash verifiable
4
Parcel Marked Completed — Dashboard Updates
Parcel card in the left panel turns dark (Completed). Dashboard KPIs update: "Opinions Signed" count increments, "Pending" count decrements. Full audit trail remains accessible but immutable. Data retained for 7 years per policy.
